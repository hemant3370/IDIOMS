Idioms: out and out -- out of circulation


 
IdiomMeaningExample
out and out bold, not hidden, a bare-faced lie The advertisement was an out-and-out lie. It contained no truth.
out cold unconscious, knocked out Liam is out cold. A baseball hit him on the side of the head.
out for blood trying to defeat or hurt, seeking revenge The Jets are out for blood tonight because they lost 7-0 last night.
out in left field not logical or sensible, all wet Don't listen to his advice. He's out in left field.
out in the cold not included in a group, not invited He was left out in the cold because of his religious beliefs.
out loud loud enough to hear, spoken clearly Dad was very angry, but he didn't swear out loud.
out of have no more, have none, run out Stop at the bakery, please. We're out of bread.
out of bounds off the playing field, across boundary lines Ramone kicked the soccer ball over my head and out of bounds.
out of breath short of breath, puffing, out of breath She was out of breath after climbing the stairs.
out of circulation not visiting, don't get around I've been out of circulation. I've been studying.
