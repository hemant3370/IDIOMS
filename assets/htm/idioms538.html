Idioms: stick them up -- stitch in time


 
IdiomMeaningExample
stick them up put your hands above your head, hands up The man took out a gun and said to the clerk, "Stick 'em up!"
stick to it stay at a tough job, hang in there If you stick to it - practice every day - you will be a fine pianist.
stick to your guns not change your belief, be firm If you testify in court, stick to your guns. Tell the same story.
stick up for support, encourage Will you stick up for me if I get in an argument?
stiff not pay for, leave without paying The cab driver told a policeman that a lady had stiffed him.
stiff person, man You lucky stiff! You won a TV!
stiff upper lip (See keep a stiff upper lip)
still wet behind the ears still quite young, the tender age of When we moved to Boston, you were still wet behind the ears.
stink the joint out play very poorly, perform badly In Chicago, we stunk the joint out, losing 7-1 to the Hawks.
stitch in time (See a stitch in time)
